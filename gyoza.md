# Gyoza
## Japanese Dumplings (餃子)
_Japanese_, _Asian_, _pan-fried_, _steamed_, _pork_  
Preparation time: 60 mins + 30 mins forming  
Makes 40–50 dumplings  

## Ingredients
* 450 g (1 lb) Napa cabbage, finely minced (about 1/2 a medium head)
* 1 tablespoon kosher salt, divided
* 450 g (1 lb) ground pork shoulder
* 1 teaspoon white pepper
* 1 tablespoon fresh garlic, minced (about 3 medium cloves)
* 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
* 55 g (2 oz) scallions, minced (about 3 whole scallions)
* 2 teaspoons sugar
* 1 package dumpling wrappers (40–50 wrappers)
* Vegetable or canola oil, for cooking

## Preparation
1. Combine cabbage and 2 teaspoons of the salt in a large bowl and toss to mix. Transfer to a fine-mesh strainer set over the bowl. Let stand at room temperature for 15 minutes to draw out moisture.
2. Transfer cabbage to the centre of a clean dish towel, gather the edges and twist firmly to squeeze out as much excess moisture as possible. Discard the liquid.
3. Combine pork, drained cabbage, remaining teaspoon of salt, white pepper, garlic, ginger, scallions and sugar in a large bowl. Knead and turn with clean hands until the mixture is homogeneous and starting to feel tacky/sticky. To test seasoning: take a teaspoon of filling and microwave for about 10 seconds until cooked through. Taste and adjust salt, white pepper and/or sugar.
4. Set up a work station: small bowl of water, a clean cloth for wiping fingers, the bowl of filling, a parchment-lined rimmed baking sheet for finished dumplings, and a stack of wrappers covered in plastic wrap.
5. **To form dumplings:** Hold one wrapper on a flat hand. Place about 1–1.5 tablespoons of filling in the centre of the wrapper. Lightly moisten the edge of the wrapper with water using a fingertip (not too much). Wipe finger dry on the cloth. Fold the wrapper into a crescent shape, pleating the edge as you seal. (Search for YouTube videos showing this technique — it takes practice.) Transfer to the lined baking sheet.
6. **At this point, dumplings can be frozen:** Place baking sheet in freezer for at least 30 minutes, then transfer to a zip-lock freezer bag. Cook directly from frozen. Dumplings keep for up to 2 months.
7. **To cook:** Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a medium non-stick skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add as many dumplings as fit in a single layer. Cook until evenly golden-brown on the bottom, about 1.5 minutes.
8. Increase heat to medium-high. Add 1/2 cup (120 ml) water and cover tightly with a lid. Steam for 3 minutes (5 minutes if frozen). Remove lid and allow remaining water to evaporate until the bottoms are crispy again.
9. Serve with soy sauce, rice vinegar and chili oil for dipping.
