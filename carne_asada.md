# Carne Asada
_Mexican_, _BBQ_, _grill_, _beef_  
Preparation time: 30 mins + 2 hours marinating  
4 portions  

## Ingredients
* 1/2 cup olive oil, plus more for oiling grill
* 1 tablespoon whole cumin seeds
* 4 garlic cloves, smashed
* 3/4 cup packed fresh cilantro leaves, divided
* 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
* 1 tablespoon soy sauce
* 2 teaspoons ancho chile powder
* 2 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
* 1 teaspoon honey
* 1 1/2 pounds skirt steak, cut crosswise into 2 equal pieces
* Lime wedges and pico de gallo, for serving

## Preparation
* Heat oil, cumin, and garlic in a small skillet over medium heat. Cook, stirring often, until fragrant and garlic is lightly browned, 3–4 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool 5 minutes.
* Combine oil mixture, 1/2 cup cilantro, lime juice, soy sauce, chile powder, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, and honey in a blender. Process until smooth, about 30 seconds.
* Reserve 1 tablespoon of the marinade. Pour remaining marinade over the skirt steak pieces in a large zip-lock bag. Seal and shake to coat evenly. Refrigerate for 2 hours.
* Remove steaks from marinade and place on a rimmed baking sheet lined with paper towels (do not rub off excess marinade). Blot the surface with paper towels, then brush with a thin layer of neutral oil to help form a good char. Sprinkle both sides with remaining 3/4 teaspoon salt. Let stand at room temperature while preheating grill.
* Preheat charcoal grill to high (450–500°F / 230–260°C). Place steaks on oiled grill grate. Grill uncovered until charred and cooked to desired doneness, about 2 minutes per side for medium-rare.
* Let steak rest 5 minutes on a cutting board with a juice channel. Slice against the grain.
* Stir together board juices and the reserved tablespoon of marinade; drizzle over sliced steak. Sprinkle with remaining 1/4 cup cilantro. Serve with lime wedges and pico de gallo.

### Enjoy!
