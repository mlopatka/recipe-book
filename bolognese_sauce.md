# Bolognese Sauce
_Italian_, _pasta_, _beef_, _pork_

4–6 portions  

## Ingredients
* 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
* 1 medium onion, finely diced
* 2 medium carrots, finely diced
* 1 medium celery rib, finely diced
* 100 g thickly sliced spek (smoked pancetta / bacon)
* 250 g (1/2 pound) ground beef
* 250 g (1/2 pound) ground pork
* 2 large garlic cloves, chopped
* 1 bottle dry red wine
* 6 peeled Italian tomatoes, seeded and finely chopped, juices reserved
* 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
* 1 bay leaf
* Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
* 1/4 cup heavy cream
* 2 pounds penne rigate (or pasta of choice)
* Freshly grated Parmesan, for serving

## Preparation
⚠️ **Instructions were not included in the source file.** Standard Bolognese method:

* Heat olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium-high heat. Add onion, carrot and celery; cook until softened, about 8 minutes.
* Add the spek and cook for 3 minutes. Add ground beef and pork; break up the meat and cook until no longer pink.
* Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Pour in the red wine; cook, stirring, until most of the liquid has evaporated.
* Add tomatoes with their juices, thyme and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper.
* Simmer on low heat for 2–3 hours, stirring occasionally, until sauce is thick and rich.
* Stir in heavy cream. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove bay leaf.
* Serve over pasta with freshly grated Parmesan.
