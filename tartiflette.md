# Tartiflette
_French_, _Savoyard_, _Alpine_, _oven_, _potato_, _cheese_  
Preparation time: 30 mins + 20–25 mins baking  
4–6 portions  

## Ingredients
* 1.2 kg potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters
* 200 g smoked lardons (or smoked streaky bacon cut into small pieces)
* 2 large pink or red onions, peeled and diced (or 10 pink shallots)
* 2 cloves garlic, peeled and finely diced
* 150 ml dry white wine
* 1 × 500 g Reblochon cheese
* 6 tablespoons crème fraîche
* Butter, for greasing
* Salt and pepper, to taste

## Preparation
1. Pre-heat oven to 200°C (400°F / Gas mark 6). Butter an ovenproof gratin dish or shallow casserole dish.
2. Boil potatoes until just soft. Drain and allow to cool before cutting into slices.
3. Meanwhile, fry the lardons (or bacon pieces), onions and garlic together until the lardons are crisp and the onions and garlic are soft and translucent.
4. Add half of the wine to the lardon and onion mixture, turn the heat up and deglaze for 2–3 minutes until most of the wine has reduced.
5. Add the cooked potato slices to the lardon and onion mixture and gently mix together. Spoon half of the mixture into the prepared dish.
6. Cut the Reblochon cheese in half through the centre (horizontally), then cut the two halves into cubes.
7. Scatter half of the Reblochon cubes over the lardon and potato mixture, crust side up.
8. Spoon the remaining potato and lardon mixture on top. Scatter the rest of the Reblochon cubes over the top, crust side up again.
9. Pour the remaining wine and the crème fraîche over the top.
10. Bake for 20–25 minutes until the cheese has melted and the tartiflette is golden brown and bubbling.
11. Serve hot from the oven with green salad, cornichons (gherkins), pickled onions, charcuterie and crusty bread.
