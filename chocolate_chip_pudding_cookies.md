# Chocolate Chip Pudding Cookies
_American_, _baked_goods_, _cookies_, _dessert_  
Preparation time: 15 mins + 10–12 mins baking  
Makes ~14 large cookies  

## Ingredients
* 2 1/4 cups (288 g) all-purpose flour
* 1 teaspoon (5 g) baking soda
* 1/2 teaspoon (3 g) salt
* 1 cup (227 g) unsalted butter, softened
* 3/4 cup (165 g) brown sugar
* 1/4 cup (50 g) granulated sugar
* 1 package (105 g / 3.7 oz) vanilla instant pudding mix
* 2 large eggs
* 1 teaspoon (5 g) vanilla extract
* 2 cups (350 g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

**If no pudding mix is available, substitute with:**
* 3 tablespoons (25 g) cornstarch
* 1/2 cup (100 g) white sugar
* 1 extra teaspoon (5 g) vanilla extract

## Preparation
1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the baking sheet in the freezer while you prepare the dough.
2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda and salt. Set aside.
3. Using a stand mixer or electric hand mixer, beat butter and both sugars on high speed until light and creamy — at least 3 minutes. Do not skip this step.
4. Add the pudding mix (or cornstarch + extra sugar + vanilla substitute), vanilla extract and eggs to the butter mixture. Beat on high for 2–3 minutes.
5. Slowly add half of the dry ingredients to the wet mixture. Start the mixer on low to prevent flour flying, then increase to high and mix until combined. Repeat with remaining dry ingredients until dough is just combined. Stir in chocolate chips with a rubber spatula.
6. Drop cookies by 1/4-cup portions (~70 g each) onto the chilled prepared baking sheet. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until slightly golden and just set on top.
7. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for 2 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

### Enjoy!

> **For chewier cookies:** Slightly underbake them (pull out when just set on top). While still warm, hold the baking tray about 30 cm above your counter and drop it — this makes the cookies collapse and become dense and chewy rather than cakey.
> **Variation:** Add peanut butter chips in addition to or instead of some of the chocolate chips. Vanilla bean paste can be substituted for vanilla extract for a more intense flavour.
