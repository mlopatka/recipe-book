# Turkey Roasting Guide
_American_, _Thanksgiving_, _poultry_, _oven_  
Preparation time: 30 mins + variable roasting time (see below)  
Portions: variable  

## Ingredients

**Sage butter:**
* Unsalted butter (generous amount)
* Fresh sage leaves, finely chopped
* Salt and pepper

**Maple glaze (for basting):**
* Maple syrup
* Bacon drippings (reserved from stuffing preparation)
* A little hot water to thin the glaze

**Giblet stock (for gravy):**
* Reserved turkey giblets and organs
* 3 crushed garlic cloves
* 3 cups water

**Gravy (from drippings):**
* Pan drippings from the roasting pan
* Flour (for thickening)
* Giblet stock

## Roasting Times

| Turkey weight | Total time |
|---|---|
| 10.2 kg | ~6 hrs 5 mins |
| 5.5 kg (2 × 5.5 kg) | ~4 hrs |

**For a 10.2 kg turkey:**
* 2 hours at 180°C (covered)
* 3 hours at 160°C (covered)
* 40 minutes grill/uncovered
* 25 minutes resting in turned-off oven

**For 2 × 5.5 kg turkeys:**
* 75 minutes at 180°C (covered)
* 120 minutes at 160°C (covered)
* 40 minutes grill/uncovered
* 20–30 minutes resting

## Preparation
1. Preheat oven to 160°C and remove the top rack.
2. In a bowl, combine softened butter and finely chopped fresh sage. Mash with a fork until well combined; season with salt and pepper.
3. Rinse the bird thoroughly inside and out with cold water and pat dry.
4. Sprinkle the cavity and skin liberally with salt and pepper.
5. Using your fingers, gently lift the skin from the breast and legs. Slip pieces of the sage butter underneath and massage it in.
6. Fill the cavity with stuffing (do not pack too tightly). Truss the turkey. Place on a rack in a large roasting pan.
7. Put into the preheated oven covered with foil.
8. **Make giblet stock:** Simmer giblets and crushed garlic in 3 cups of water in a small covered pan for 3 hours.
9. Whisk together the maple syrup, bacon drippings and a little hot water for the glaze. Baste the turkey every 30 minutes.
10. Follow the roasting time guide above. The turkey is done when juices run clear and the thickest part of thigh and breast is piping hot with no pink meat remaining.
11. Remove foil for the final 30–40 minutes to brown the skin.
12. Rest covered with foil for at least 30 minutes before carving.
13. **Make gravy:** Skim excess fat from pan drippings. Place roasting pan over 2 burners on medium-high. Scrape up browned bits with a wooden spoon. Whisk in flour until thick. Add giblet stock and stir until smooth.

### Enjoy!
