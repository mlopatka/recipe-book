# Barszcz Czerwony
## Polish Red Borscht
_Polish_, _soup_, _vegetarian-optional_, _beet_  
Preparation time: 60–90 mins  
6–8 portions  

## Ingredients
* 4 beets
* 3 quarts beef broth (or vegetable broth for vegetarian)
* 1 ham bone (omit for Christmas Eve / vegetarian version)
* 2 parsnips
* 2 bay leaves
* 2 onions, chopped
* Small box of chopped mushrooms
* 2 apples, chopped
* 1 large leek
* Bunch of fresh parsley
* Juice of 1 whole lemon
* 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
* 2 teaspoons cumin
* 2 tablespoons dill
* 2 teaspoons thyme
* Garlic, to taste
* Salt and pepper, to taste

## Preparation
1. Carefully wash the beets, peel and slice thinly. Add to a large stock pot with the ham bone (if using), beef broth and bay leaves. Simmer for 20–30 minutes.
2. While soup is simmering, chop the remaining vegetables. Add parsnips, onions, mushrooms, apples, leek, parsley, lemon juice, vinegar, cumin, dill and thyme to the soup. Add more broth to cover all vegetables if needed. Simmer slowly for a further 30 minutes.
3. Remove the ham bone, pick off any small pieces of meat and return them to the soup.
4. If you prefer a creamier soup, use a hand blender to blend to desired thickness. For a chunkier broth-style borscht, skip blending.
5. Taste and season with salt, pepper, dill and vinegar as needed.

### Smacznego!

> **Tip:** A wonderful shortcut for the fermented tang that traditional borscht gets from fermented beet juice — add finely chopped sauerkraut (made with only water and salt) plus a splash of its juice to the pot at the end.
