# Moroccan Lamb Stew
_Moroccan_, _North African_, _lamb_, _stew_, _slow-cook_  
Preparation time: ⚠️ TODO — not recorded in source  
4–6 portions  

## Ingredients
* Extra-virgin olive oil
* 2 large yellow or white onions
* 4–5 cloves of garlic
* 500–600 g lamb, cut into 3 cm cubes (bone-in or boneless), trimmed
* 2 red bell peppers (paprika)
* 1 can (~300 ml) green pitted olives
* 1 can (~300 ml) black pitted olives
* 150 g dried dates, pitted
* 150 g dried prunes, pitted
* 1 small tin of tomato paste
* 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves
* 1 teaspoon ground cumin
* 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
* 1 medium tomato, diced
* Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

**To serve:** Brown rice or buckwheat

> **Note:** The volume of lamb should approximately equal the combined volume of dates, prunes and olives.

## Preparation
⚠️ **Instructions were not included in the source file.** Standard Moroccan stew method:

* Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium-high heat. Season lamb with salt and pepper. Brown lamb pieces on all sides; set aside.
* In the same pot, sauté onions until softened. Add garlic, cumin and cinnamon; cook 1 minute.
* Return lamb to the pot. Add tomato paste, diced tomato and bell peppers.
* Add olives, dates and prunes. Pour in enough water or stock to just cover the ingredients.
* Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer covered for 1.5–2 hours until lamb is very tender.
* Stir in fresh coriander. Taste and adjust seasoning.
* Serve over brown rice or buckwheat.

### Enjoy!
