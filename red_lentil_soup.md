# Rode Linzensoep
## Red Lentil Soup with Smoked Sausage
_Dutch_, _soup_, _lentil_, _smoked-sausage_  
Preparation time: 20 mins + 60 mins simmering  
6–8 portions  

## Ingredients
* 4 cloves garlic (teentjes knoflook), minced
* 2 large winter carrots (winterwortels), diced
* 3 stalks celery (bleekselderij), diced
* 1 leek (prei), sliced
* 100 g smoked bacon strips (rookspekreepjes)
* 3 small very dry chilis, ground
* 140 g tomato paste (tomatenpuree, 1 tin)
* 2 litres water + 1 bouillon cube (or 2 litres stock)
* 500 g orange/red lentils (oranje linzen), unsoaked
* 2 bay leaves (laurierblad)
* 1 smoked sausage — slager's rookworst (butcher's quality if possible)
* Olive oil, for cooking
* Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste

## Preparation
* Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat. Add the bacon and slowly render until beginning to crisp.
* Add the garlic and chili flakes; fry for 1 minute.
* Add the winter carrot, celery and leek. Increase the heat slightly and sauté, stirring, for a few minutes until the vegetables start to colour.
* Add the tomato paste and cook for a further few minutes to remove the raw tinned flavour.
* Pour in the bouillon (water + cube, or stock). Add the unsoaked lentils, rosemary sprig and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
* Reduce heat, place the lid ajar on the pot and let the soup simmer for about 1 hour. The lentils should break down completely.
* Meanwhile, cook the rookworst according to the butcher's instructions — usually 15–20 minutes in water just below boiling. Slice into rounds and stir into the soup.
* Taste and season generously with salt and freshly ground pepper.
* Serve with warm stokbrood (baguette) or rye bread and katenspek.
