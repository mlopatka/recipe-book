# Ciasto Serowo-Makowe
## Polish Poppy Seed & Cheesecake
_Polish_, _baked_goods_, _dessert_, _cake_  
Preparation time: 45 mins + 60 mins baking  
10–12 portions  

## Ingredients

**Base (cookie crust):**
* 200 g cookie crumbs (e.g., digestive biscuits or shortbread)
* 1/2 block (125 g) butter, melted

**Poppy seed filling layer:**
* 400 g poppy seed filling (milled poppy seeds), or make your own from:
  * Milled poppy seeds
  * 60 ml Cointreau (or orange liqueur)
  * Water
  * Orange zest
  * Raisins
* 3 egg yolks
* 3/4 cup powdered sugar
* 1/2 cup milk (or evaporated milk, to achieve a good consistency)

**Cheese filling layer:**
* 700 g milled white curd cheese (twaróg / quark)
* 3 eggs, separated
* 1/2 block (125 g) butter, softened
* 3/4 cup powdered sugar
* 1 tablespoon cornstarch (thickener)
* 50 g raisins
* 50 g nuts (walnuts, pecans or hazelnuts), roughly chopped

## Preparation
1. **Make the base:** Mix cookie crumbs with melted butter until combined. Press firmly into the base of a lined springform tin. Refrigerate while preparing the fillings.
2. **Prepare the poppy layer:** If using ready-made poppy seed filling, combine it with the Cointreau, orange zest and raisins in a bowl. Beat egg yolks with powdered sugar until pale. Fold the egg yolk mixture into the poppy seed filling. Add milk as needed for a smooth, spreadable consistency.
3. **Prepare the cheese layer:** Beat egg yolks with powdered sugar until pale and fluffy. In a separate bowl, combine softened butter, curd cheese, cornstarch and the egg yolk mixture until smooth. Fold in raisins and chopped nuts. In a clean bowl, beat the egg whites to stiff peaks, then gently fold into the cheese mixture.
4. **Assemble:** Spread the poppy seed layer over the cookie base. Gently spoon and spread the cheese layer on top.
5. **Bake:** Bake at 170°C (340°F) for 10 minutes, then reduce temperature to 160°C (320°F) and bake for a further 50 minutes until set (a skewer inserted in the centre should come out mostly clean).
6. Allow to cool completely in the tin before slicing. Best served chilled.
