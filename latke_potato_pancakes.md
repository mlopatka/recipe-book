# Latkes — Classic Potato Pancakes
_Jewish_, _Polish_, _vegetarian_, _potato_, _pan-fried_  
Preparation time: 30 mins  
Makes ~24 pancakes  

## Ingredients
* 1 medium onion, peeled
* 4 large russet or Idaho potatoes (about 1.6 kg / 3.5 lb), peeled
* 2 large eggs
* 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
* 6 tablespoons vegetable oil
* 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
* Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
* Applesauce and/or sour cream, for serving

## Preparation
1. Preheat oven to 95°C (200°F). Place 2 nonstick baking sheets in the oven to warm.
2. Using a box grater or food processor with grating disc, coarsely grate the onion and place in a colander set over the sink. Coarsely grate the potatoes, add to the colander, and set aside to drain for a few minutes.
3. In a large mixing bowl, lightly beat the eggs, then whisk in the flour.
4. Press the potatoes and onion firmly to extract as much liquid as possible, then add to the egg/flour mixture. Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Mix well but do not overwork.
5. Heat 1 tablespoon oil and 1 tablespoon butter in a heavy-bottomed 30 cm (12-inch) skillet over moderately high heat until hot but not smoking. Drop 4 scant 1/4-cup portions of potato mixture into the pan and flatten with a spatula to form 8 cm (3-inch) pancakes.
6. Fry until bottoms are golden-brown, 4–5 minutes. Flip and fry until golden-brown and crisp on the other side, a further 4–5 minutes. Transfer to paper towels to drain; season immediately with salt and pepper.
7. Keep warm on the baking sheets in the oven while making remaining batches.
8. Wipe out the pan with paper towels between batches. Add 1 tablespoon oil and 1 tablespoon butter before each new batch. Repeat until all batter is used.
9. Serve hot with applesauce and/or sour cream.

### Enjoy!
