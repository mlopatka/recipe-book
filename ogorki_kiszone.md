# Ogórki Kiszone
## Polish Lacto-Fermented Pickles
_Polish_, _preserving_, _fermentation_, _vegetarian_, _condiment_  
Preparation time: 20 mins + 3–7 days fermentation  
Makes: 2–3 jars  

## Ingredients

**Cucumbers & aromatics:**
* Cucumbers (ogórki) — pickling variety, quantity to fill your jars
* Garlic (czosnek) — 2 cloves per jar
* Fresh dill (koper) — to taste
* Horseradish (chrzan) — 3 pieces per jar, peeled and sliced

**Brine (zalewa) — per 1 litre jar:**
* 1 litre boiled water, cooled to room temperature (zimnej — cold)
* 10 ml white vinegar
* 1 tablespoon salt

**Optional aromatics:**
* 1–2 cherry tree leaves (wiśni)
* Black peppercorns
* Oak leaves

## Preparation
1. Wash cucumbers and fresh dill thoroughly. Peel and slice the garlic and horseradish.
2. Pack the cucumbers upright in sterilised glass jars, with the lighter-coloured ends pointing upward.
3. Add to each jar: 2 pieces of garlic, 3 slices of horseradish, and dill to taste. Optionally add 1–2 cherry leaves, peppercorns and/or oak leaves for extra flavour complexity.
4. Prepare the brine: combine the cooled boiled water, vinegar and salt and stir until the salt dissolves.
5. Fill each jar to the top with the cold brine. Screw the lids on tightly.
6. Leave at room temperature to ferment. Pickles will be lightly fermented after 3 days and more sour after 5–7 days. Move to the refrigerator once you reach the desired sourness.

### Smacznego!

> **Note:** These are traditional Polish kiszone (lacto-fermented), not shelf-stable vinegar pickles. The small amount of vinegar here is a shortcut — traditional recipes use only salt brine and rely entirely on lacto-fermentation. Taste daily after day 3 to monitor sourness.
