# Fennel and Lemon Risotto
_Italian_, _vegetarian_, _risotto_, _stovetop_  
Preparation time: 50 mins  
4 portions  

## Ingredients
* 1 large fennel bulb (about 400 g)
* 2 litres vegetable or light chicken stock (or homemade — see note)
* 75 g cold butter
* 1 small white onion, or 2 shallots, chopped
* 320 g risotto rice (ideally Carnaroli or Vialone Nano)
* 125 ml dry white wine
* Zest of 1 small unwaxed lemon
* 60 g Parmesan, grated
* Pinch of salt

## Preparation
1. Trim the fennel bulb, removing the thicker outer layer and finger-like stalks. Set aside any feathery fronds for decoration. *To make your own stock:* place fennel trimmings with other vegetables and 2 litres of water in a pot; bring to the boil, reduce to a simmer for 40 minutes, then strain. Keep the stock warm on the back of the stove.
2. Slice the trimmed fennel bulb into slim arcs. In a large deep frying pan or heavy saucepan, melt half the butter over medium heat. Gently fry the onion (or shallots) and fennel with a pinch of salt until soft and translucent.
3. Add the rice, stirring until every grain glistens and is heated through. Add the wine — it should sizzle as it hits the pan. Stir until absorbed.
4. Start adding stock a ladleful at a time, stirring firmly and allowing each ladleful to be absorbed before adding the next. The process will take about 16 minutes. The rice is cooked when it is tender but still has bite, and the risotto is still loose and rippling rather than stiff.
5. Turn off the heat. Add the remaining butter, the Parmesan and lemon zest. Cover the pan. Let sit for 1 minute, then beat vigorously until the risotto is creamy and glossy.
6. Taste and add salt if needed. Divide between plates, decorating with reserved fennel fronds and a little extra lemon zest.
